Overview

An introduction to the science behind black holes

Black holes are regions in space where gravity is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape their pull. They are formed when massive stars collapse under their own gravity at the end of their life cycle.

Types of Black Holes

Different categories based on their mass and origin

Stellar Black Holes

Formed by the gravitational collapse of massive stars. Typically have masses up to 20 times that of the Sun.

Supermassive Black Holes

Found at the centers of galaxies, including the Milky Way. They can have millions or billions of solar masses.

Intermediate Black Holes

A rare class with masses between stellar and supermassive black holes. They may form from star cluster collisions.
